About the VillAGE Lab

The VillAGE Lab (Lifecourse Research on Aging Across Contexts) is dedicated to understanding the complex interplay between individual aging processes and the diverse contexts that shape them. Our research spans multiple disciplines, examining how social, cultural, environmental, and economic factors influence health and well-being throughout the lifespan.

We employ innovative methodologies to study aging as a dynamic process that unfolds differently across various settings and populations. Our work contributes to evidence-based policies and interventions that promote healthy aging for all individuals, regardless of their background or circumstances.

Our Research Focus

Lifecourse Perspectives: Understanding aging as a lifelong process
Contextual Factors: Examining social, cultural, and environmental influences
Health Disparities: Addressing inequalities in aging outcomes
Intervention Development: Creating evidence-based solutions

Social Determinants of Aging

Investigating how social factors such as education, income, social support, and community resources influence aging trajectories and health outcomes across different populations.

Cultural Context & Aging

Examining how cultural values, beliefs, and practices shape experiences of aging, with particular attention to diverse ethnic and cultural communities.

Environmental Gerontology

Studying the relationship between physical and built environments and aging well-being, including housing, neighborhood design, and accessibility.

Intergenerational Studies

Exploring relationships across generations and how intergenerational connections impact health, social support, and quality of life in later years.
